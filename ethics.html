ETHICS RULES — OPERATIONAL CONDUCT

1. Every operative is accountable for their actions at all times, regardless of orders, circumstances, or outcome.

2. Ignorance of ethical directives does not exempt an operative from punishment.

3. Orders that clearly violate ethics must be reported immediately to Oversight.

4. Blind obedience is not a defense for unethical conduct.

5. Civilian lives are to be protected as a priority unless explicitly overridden by Oversight.

6. Excessive force is prohibited when a lesser action achieves the same objective.

7. Prisoners and detainees must not be harmed, abused, or exploited.

8. Medical personnel must be protected and allowed to perform their duties.

9. Looting, theft, or unauthorized acquisition of property is forbidden.

10. Personal vendettas must never influence operational decisions.

11. Collateral damage must be minimized at all costs.

12. Collateral damage must be reported truthfully and in full.

13. Falsifying reports is a severe ethical violation.

14. Altering evidence is prohibited.

15. Tampering with recordings, logs, or data is prohibited.

16. Friendly fire incidents must be reported immediately.

17. Covering for another operative’s misconduct is punishable.

18. Loyalty to teammates does not override loyalty to ethics.

19. Abuse of authority is forbidden.

20. Threats, coercion, or intimidation outside mission scope are prohibited.

21. Operatives must not act under the influence of substances.

22. Reckless behavior that endangers teammates is unethical.

23. Unauthorized execution of targets is forbidden.

24. Rules of engagement must be followed precisely.

25. Deviations from mission parameters require justification.

26. Medical aid must be rendered when feasible, even to adversaries.

27. Torture is prohibited under all circumstances.

28. Psychological abuse is prohibited.

29. Sexual misconduct is prohibited.

30. Exploitation of civilians is prohibited.

31. Use of prohibited weapons is forbidden.

32. Unauthorized experimentation on subjects is forbidden.

33. Maintain operational readiness at all times.

34. Ensure proper maintenance and accountability of all equipment.

35. Never compromise a mission for personal convenience.

36. Avoid unnecessary risks that could endanger teammates.

37. Do not engage in conflicts of interest.

38. Respect cultural norms and local populations during operations.

39. Avoid reprisals or retaliation outside of lawful engagement.

40. Conduct yourself professionally during all interactions with allied forces.

41. Maintain accurate and timely logs of operational activities.

42. Ensure proper handling and disposal of classified or sensitive material.

43. Respect privacy rights of civilians and non-combatants.

44. Do not falsify reports, intelligence, or operational outcomes.

45. Avoid intimidation, harassment, or abuse of authority.

46. Report misconduct by peers or superiors through proper channels.

47. Protect mission-critical assets from theft or sabotage.

48. Prioritize the safety and security of teammates in all planning.

49. Follow proper chains of command in every decision.

50. Train continuously to maintain professional competency.

51. Respect and uphold international humanitarian standards.

52. Avoid unnecessary destruction of property unless operationally required.

53. Ensure that all actions can withstand post-mission ethical review.

54. Refrain from using classified or operational knowledge for personal gain.

55. Foster trust and morale among team members.

56. Maintain situational awareness at all times.

57. Avoid involvement in political disputes unrelated to operational duties.

58. Strive for transparency and honesty in all operational reporting.
